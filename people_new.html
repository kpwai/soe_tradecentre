Faculty Members from SMU

Lead PI: CHANG Pao Li

CHANG Pao Li is Associate Professor of Economics and Associate Dean (Research) at SMU. Her areas of research expertise include empirical and quantitative analyses of trade flows and labor market response, empirical analyses of global value chain, and analyses of firm-level micro performances. She will take charge of the data acquisition process and the communication with public/private sources for obtaining the data. She will spearhead research projects under the research themes of productivity estimation, GVC, trade/labor, and semiconductor industry. She has been PI for several research grants, including an MOE Tier 2 grant. She is full-time at SMU and will devote 35% effort to the project.

Co-PI: MA Lin

MA Lin is Assistant Professor of Economics at SMU. His main research areas include trade and economic geography. Lin is very experienced in quantitative analyses of large-scale spatial models and global sourcing. He will supervise the data compilation / maintenance activities, oversee the projects under the research themes of innovation, and lead the project related to transportation infrastructure. He is full-time at SMU and will devote 25% effort to the project.

Co-PI: Jing LI

Jing LI is Associate Professor of Economics at SMU. She specializes in urban economics and has conducted extensive research on the spatial economics of innovation activities. She will lead the projects related to remote working, and contribute to projects under the research theme of innovation. She is full-time at SMU and will devote 15% effort to the project.

Collaborator: Zhenlin YANG

Zhenlin YANG is Professor of Economics and Statistics at SMU, with strong expertise in Spatial Econometrics. Zhenlin will contribute to the research activities in developing productivity estimation methodologies, given firm-to-firm linkages. The lead PI has an on-going project with Zhenlin in this regard. He is full-time at SMU and will devote 10% effort to the project.

Collaborator: Yuan MEI

Yuan MEI is Assistant Professor of Economics at SMU. His areas of research expertise include trade policy, spatial economics, and development economics. He will contribute to the empirical and quantitative analyses of firm-level responses to trade policy shocks. He is full-time at SMU and will devote 10% effort to the project.

Collaborator: Chenying YANG

Chenying YANG is Assistant Professor of Economics at SMU. Her research specializes in international trade, industrial organization, and spatial economics. She will complement the team with her expertise in empirical identification of high-dimensional interactions among plants/firms, and in industrial policies for global value chains. She is full-time at SMU and will devote 10% effort to the project.
